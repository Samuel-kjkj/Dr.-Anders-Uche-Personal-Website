About Me

Anders’ wide ranging global health interests mirror the complexity in the global health space, with granular and global (bottom-up) experiences from the clinical, sub-national , national and international contexts give him unique perspectives on a variety of health issues (Oral health, Vaccines, Access to medicines, Malaria, TB and HIV). He previously worked with the Nigerian Government at WHO-Inter-country center for Oral Health (ICOH) now RCORTI for Africa in Jos, Nigeria; the WHO HQ, the Global Health Center (a global health research think tank in Switzerland); GAVI (Global Alliance for Vaccines and Immunizations); the Foundation for Innovative New Diagnostics (FIND), and the Global Fund (GFATM). He focused on oral health and social determinants of health, Malaria prevention, diagnosis and treatment (MTGs), access to medicines, Vaccine implementation (Measles and Rubella), ehealth/CHWs and strategic and thematic evaluations respectively. Currently, he is with Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance where he primarily focuses on evaluation and measurements (strategic and thematic reviews) that informs the Gavi's strategy, business model, investments and impact as well as policy, programme and project management activities. He is interested in health policy, measurements, strategy, global health governance, international development and health systems.
